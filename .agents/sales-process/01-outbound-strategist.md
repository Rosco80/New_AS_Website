## SECTION 1: ICP TIER BREAKDOWN

- Size: 5–30 employees, 1–3 locations
- Tools: Fresha, Mindbody, Vagaro, or homegrown WhatsApp booking; often no real CRM
- Revenue: $300K–$2M/year

**Primary Title:** Owner / Founder / Clinic Director
**Secondary Title:** Practice Manager, Studio Manager

**Pain Point Angle:** "You're losing booked appointments to competitors who respond in 3 seconds. Your receptionist can't work 24/7. An AI can."

**Best Channel Mix:** Cold Email (primary), LinkedIn (warm), Instagram DM (vertical-native), Referral from existing wellness client in Malaysia (highest-trust)

## SECTION 2: BUYING SIGNAL INVENTORY

**Signal 1: Hiring a Receptionist, Admin Assistant, or VA**
Where to find it: LinkedIn Jobs alerts, Indeed RSS feeds, Google Alerts
Outreach angle: "Before you spend $30K/year on salary — there's a faster and cheaper way to cover that workload. Worth 20 minutes?"

**Signal 2: New Location or Expansion Announcement**
Where to find it: LinkedIn company posts, Google News alerts, Instagram announcements
Outreach angle: "Expanding to a new location is exciting — and operationally brutal. Most founders don't realize their admin load doubles before their revenue does."

**Signal 3: Negative Google Reviews Mentioning Slow Response**
Where to find it: Google Maps reviews (search target business type, sort by recent)
Outreach angle: "I noticed a few recent reviews mentioning response time at [business]. That's almost always a systems gap, not a people problem."

**Signal 4: Active Paid Social Advertising**
Where to find it: Facebook Ad Library (free, publicly searchable)
Outreach angle: "You're paying to drive leads — are those leads being captured 24/7? Most SMEs lose 40% of paid traffic after hours."

**Signal 5: LinkedIn Post About Being Overwhelmed**
Where to find it: LinkedIn feed, Sales Navigator keyword alerts
Outreach angle: Engage with the post first (genuine comment). Then message: "Your post about [X] resonated — that's exactly the moment most founders find us."

**Signal 6: Tech Stack Showing Manual Gaps**
Where to find it: BuiltWith.com, LinkedIn company "About" tech mentions, job descriptions
Outreach angle: "Your stack tells a story — [Tool A], [Tool B], [Tool C], none of them talking to each other. That's 5–10 hours a week in manual data transfer."

**Signal 7: Job Description That Reads Like a Robot's Job**
Where to find it: LinkedIn Jobs, Indeed — tasks like "answer incoming calls," "update CRM records," "send follow-up emails," "confirm appointments"
Outreach angle: "Every task in that job description is something we automate for businesses like yours — in days, not months."

**Signal 8: Founder Engages with Automation/AI Content**
Where to find it: LinkedIn activity feed, Sales Navigator "shared content" filters
Outreach angle: "I noticed you've been engaging with automation content lately — means you're already thinking about this."

**Signal 9: Website Has Contact Form But No Live Chat**
Where to find it: Manual browse of prospect website, BuiltWith.com (absence of Intercom, Drift, Tidio), Wappalyzer browser extension
Outreach angle: "Your website is working hard to attract leads — but it stops working at 5pm."

**Signal 10: High Volume, Low Margin Business With Thin Staffing**
Where to find it: LinkedIn headcount vs. industry benchmarks, Glassdoor employer reviews
Outreach angle: "Running a lean team across high-volume operations means every manual process is a liability."

## SECTION 5: PERSONALIZATION FRAMEWORK (10-Minute Pre-Outreach Checklist)

**Step 1 — Signal Check (2 min)**
Answer: why am I reaching out to this person today? You need at least one trigger: hiring, pain-point post, running ads, new location, bad reviews. No trigger = no outreach. Come back when the signal appears.

**Step 2 — Business Snapshot (2 min)**
Visit website and LinkedIn. Note: what do they sell, how many employees, do they have a chatbot/booking automation on the website? Write 2–3 sentences about the business in your own words.

**Step 3 — Pain Point Match (2 min)**
Map to Autoflow's core pain categories:
- After-hours leads going unanswered → Engagement Engine
- Team buried in admin / CRM chaos → Operations Engine
- Phone volume eating staff time → Voice Engine
- Want to scale without hiring → Execution Engine

Pick ONE primary pain. This determines your first message.

**Step 4 — Personalization Hook (2 min)**
Find one specific, verifiable detail to open with:
- A specific post they made
- A specific review you found
- A specific job posting
- A specific location/expansion

This hook goes in line 1. It cannot apply to anyone else.

**Step 5 — CTA Selection (1 min)**
- No awareness → send ROI Calculator link
- Aware of problem, not solution → offer free AI Self Audit
- Signal-hot → direct Friction Audit booking

Never give two CTAs. One door. One step.

**Output:** A 3-line research brief: Trigger / Pain / Hook. Write the message from that brief.

**"We already use Zapier / Make."**
> That's actually a great sign — it means you've already bought into the idea that automation works. The difference is that Zapier and Make are DIY tools: they do exactly what you build, and when something breaks, it's on your team to fix it. We build, maintain, and improve the system for you — no technical overhead on your side, and we guarantee the ROI before we start.

**"Too expensive."**
> Understood — and I'd genuinely encourage you to run the numbers before deciding. Our Operations Engine at $170/month saves clients an average of 20+ hours per week. If your team's loaded cost is $15/hour, that's $1,200/week in recovered capacity for $170/month. The ROI Calculator does this math: [link].

**"We're not ready yet."**
> That's the most common thing I hear right before a business hits a growth ceiling. When you are ready, the worst time to build infrastructure is when you're already under pressure. Happy to do a no-commitment Friction Audit now so you have a roadmap ready when the moment is right.

**"We tried automation before and it didn't work."**
> That's the most important thing you could tell me. Most automation failures come from two things: tools that weren't built for your specific workflow, and no one maintaining them when something breaks. Everything we build is mapped to how your business actually operates, and we stay on as the team that keeps it running.
